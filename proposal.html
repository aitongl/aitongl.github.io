Parallel Maximum Satisfiability Solver

Aitong (Ivy) Li  •  Ananya Aggarwal

Project Website

Summary

We will build a parallel Maximum Satisfiability (MaxSAT) solver by starting from a sequential CDCL SAT solver and implementing a core-guided MaxSAT algorithm on top of it.

Workers will explore different parts of the search while selectively sharing useful information such as unsatisfiable cores, bounds, and high-quality learned clauses to reduce redundant computation.

Background

Maximum Satisfiability (MaxSAT) extends SAT by allowing some clauses to be “soft” and maximizing satisfied clauses.

Sequential algorithm:

  • Initialize bounds and relaxation variables
  • Repeat:
    • Call SAT solver
    • If satisfiable → update solution
    • If unsatisfiable → relax core
  • Until convergence

The repeated SAT solving makes this problem computationally expensive but well-suited for parallelism.

The Challenge

MaxSAT has strong dependencies across iterations, making naive parallelization ineffective.

A key challenge is deciding what information to share between workers without overwhelming communication overhead.

Resources

We will build on the Glucose SAT solver and modify it to expose internals such as learned clauses and conflicts.
